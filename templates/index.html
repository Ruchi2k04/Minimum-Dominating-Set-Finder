Abstract

The minimum dominating set problem involves finding the smallest set of nodes in a graph such that every node is either in this set or directly connected to it, making it a challenging NP-complete problem. Quantum computing offers promising solutions to such problems through the use of quantum algorithms. Grover’s search algorithm has the potential to provide faster search times compared to classical methods. This study leverages the quantum computing platform Qiskit to design and test a quantum algorithm aimed at solving the minimum dominating set problem. The proposed algorithm is experimental, producing probabilistic results and visualizing potential solutions using histograms. The goal is to develop a quantum algorithm that can outperform classical approaches in solving minimum dominating sets, showcasing the power of quantum computing for tackling complex combinatorial optimization problems.

Key Words: Quantum Computing, Qiskit, Grover’s Algorithm, Minimum Dominating Set.
